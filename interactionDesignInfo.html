Lets also talk a little bit about typography. Make sure that your type is big enough to read. Imagine putting it up on a big monitor; is the type still readable? It's reccomended to aim for 18 points (about). Make sure that there is plenty of vertical tracking between your lines of text, and that rather than spanning the whole width of the screen, you set a body text width limit so that its still readable even on wide screens.

For paragraph text, make sure thats you use a readable font! Sans serif stuff is good for this, as well as serif fonts that are simple, such as the classic Times New Roman. For headings, they can be a little more decorative than your body text, but make sure their still readable. Avoid fonts with lots of fancy glyphs.

A good way to make sure that your websit meets these standards is heurstic evaluation! This is a method of finding usability problems. You can go through the checklist yourself, but there are also professionals who go through the checklist and send back reccomnendations at many companies.
